Elevated TSH levels are commonly associated with hypothyroidism.
Low T3 levels may indicate reduced thyroid hormone production.
Abnormal TT4 values can be associated with thyroid dysfunction.
Laboratory values must be interpreted alongside patient history.
This system provides decision support and does not replace clinical judgment.

--- GENERAL PRECAUTIONS AND CARE ---

Patients with suspected thyroid dysfunction should undergo regular thyroid function monitoring as advised by clinicians.
Sudden changes in weight, heart rate, or energy levels should be reported to a healthcare professional.
Patients should avoid self-medication or altering prescribed thyroid medication without medical supervision.
Adequate iodine intake is important, but excessive supplementation should be avoided.
Stress management and regular sleep patterns may help support endocrine health.
Pregnant patients or patients planning pregnancy should receive specialized thyroid evaluation.
This system provides decision support only and does not recommend treatment.
